Who We Are

Named after Ruth Weimer Mount, the Mount Leadership Society focuses on students committed to leadership and service. Students further develop their skills by participating in a wide variety of leadership and service projects. It is an honor to be a Mount Scholar. Only 100 students are selected each year to be part of the program.

The Mount Leadership Society is guided by five "essentials" for leadership and service: Honor of Integrity, Being Prepared and Following Through, Creating and Nurturing Relationships, Valuing Individuals, and Willingness to Teach Others. Residence hall students live together their first year in program as well. Halloran House, and commuter students are part of the program as well.

Components of the Mount Leadership Society:

  • Mentoring by university staff and faculty
  • Leadership training

Honoring Ruth Weimer Mount

“Without Ruth Weimer Mount, I would not have had any of the great memories, experiences, and friendships that have helped make me into the person that I am today.”
~ Edwin Jones, Columbus, Ohio

The Mount Leadership Society is named in honor of the late Ruth Weimer Mount. Ruth joined Ohio State as the Assistant Dean of Women in 1953. Later after holding other numerous positions she was named the first Dean of Students in 1968. During her career, Ruth also served as President of National Mortar Board Inc., the Association of College Honor Societies, and the National Association of Women Deans, Administrators and Counselors.

After stepping down as Dean, Ruth continued her work in various volunteer roles for Ohio State and the community, including involvement with the OSU Hospitals Service and Executive Boards, the Alumni Association, the University’s Women’s Clubs, Planned Parenthood, Columbus Symphony Orchestra, and Franklin County Children’s Services. Of Ruth’s life and legacy, one friend said, “Ruth Weimer Mount was the consummate volunteer. All that she did was directed at improving the quality of life for others, particularly young people.” Another stated, “Ruth Mount was a unique mentor. Her guidance, grace, and leadership will serve as an example for the rest of my life.”


After Ruth’s death in 1997, the university wanted to honor her extraordinary commitment to leadership and service. While many options were considered, the university decided it would be most fitting to create a living, learning community of students to carry on her legacy of leadership and service. The Mount Leadership Society is an appropriate and fitting tribute to a woman who dedicated herself to The Ohio State University, public service, and student development throughout her extraordinary life and career.

The Mount Leadership Society was piloted with 52 students in the first class of 1999. Students were selected based on their interest in leadership and service. They were paired with a university mentor, engaged in service projects, and enhanced their leadership skills.


Based on the success of the first year, the size of the class was doubled, and now, 100 Mount Scholars are selected each year. Specialized components have been added to the program: orientation, Mount Legacy Week, Year of Service, a trip to Washington D.C., Mount Committees, and the Celebration Ceremony, just to name a few. In addition, former students and colleagues of Ruth Weimer Mount provide support and scholarships for Mount Scholars.

Program Goals


The mission of the Mount Leadership Society is to foster in our students an ethic of service and to develop leaders for a diverse society.

The goals of our program are:

  • Academic Achievement - To support a scholar's ability to succeed academically at Ohio State through learning opportunities both in and out of the classroom.
  • Community Connection - To connect scholars with the campus and the community.
  • Diversity - To increase respect for and appreciation of personal and cultural differences in a diverse society.
  • Leadership - To enhance a scholar's leadership skills and self-knowledge.
  • Service - To instill an on-going ethic of service.

What We Stand For

What does it mean to be a good leader? What does it mean to have an ethic of service? The following five competencies, drawn from Ruth Weimer Mount's personal talents and gifts, are essential to being a leader of quality. These five Mount Essentials make up the foundation of the Mount Leadership Society.

Honor of Integrity

  • Being proud to model integrity
  • Striving to create and maintain a reputation for being ethical, honest and fair
  • Sense of self-awareness
  • Acknowledgement of your own imperfections
  • Genuineness

Being Prepared and Following Through

  • Coming prepared
  • Not waiting until the last minute
  • Say what you do, do what you say
  • Fulfilling responsibilities
  • Sense of duty

Creating and Nurturing Relationships

  • Seeking commonalities
  • Valuing and maintaining relationships
  • Inviting others to make meaningful contributions
  • Involving others

Valuing Individuals

  • Respect and appreciation for abilities and limitations of others
  • Promoting diversity
  • Seeing the good in others
  • Acting with compassion and empathy
  • Benevolence

Willingness to Teach Others

  • Handing down your knowledge and experience to others
  • Caring about a better tomorrow
  • Making time for others
  • Serving as a role model

Leadership

“The unique thing about Mount and being charged to lead within the organization is that there are leaders leading leaders.”
~ Courtney Nichols, Cleveland, Ohio

Leadership is the cornerstone of the Mount Leadership Society. Mount Scholars enhance their leadership skills through regular leadership training workshops and overnight leadership retreats. Leadership Month, held in January, focuses on a series of events and activities that center on leadership at Ohio State. In addition to these events, Mount Scholars have the opportunity to discuss current event questions with former Congressman John Kasich, and travel to Washington DC to visit our nation’s capitol city.

Leadership Training

“Mount represents leadership from our involvement in Greek life to the military, from student government to residence life. Everywhere you go on campus and in our community we are able to see Mount represented through the hard work of individuals who are making the lives of those around us better one day at a time. Mount is service, leadership and action!”
~ Jonathon Glick, Orinda, California

First year Mount Scholars gather together two or three times a month for leadership training workshops. Held on Monday nights, these workshops cover a wide range of topics, including diversity, ethics, public speaking, leadership styles, and goal setting. These sessions are often led by faculty, staff, and community leaders. Scholars are asked to reserve Monday nights for Mount functions.

Leadership Month

In January, Mount Scholars have the special opportunity to take their leadership skills to another level. During Leadership Month, Scholars are presented with opportunities to meet university leaders, faculty, and staff to learn about the leadership at our university.

  • Ride along with OSU Police
  • Trip to the Rifle Range
  • Dinner at the home of an Assistant Vice President
  • Behind the scenes tour of the Woody Hayes Athletic Center
  • Lunch in President's Suite in Press Box of Ohio Stadium
  • Résumé writing workshop

Retreat

“During the retreat, I was able to ask others questions that I never thought I would ask. I think most people left feeling more like a family.”
~ Kelsi Schwall, Gahanna, Ohio

Often regarded as the most fun part of being in the Mount Leadership Society, both first and second year Scholars attend leadership retreats.

Held in the fall, the retreat for first year Scholars is a great chance for new students to learn about each other. The retreat focuses on:

  • Decision making skills
  • Diversity
  • Problem solving skills
  • Teamwork
  • Midnight skits!

The second year retreat is held in the spring and asks Scholars to reflect on their accomplishments and the legacy they wish to leave behind.

Current Events Question

Created by former Congressman John Kasich, the Current Events Question is an opportunity for first year Mount Scholars to develop their critical thinking skills by responding to current events questions generated by public servants. Scholars then gather to discuss their responses with University leaders.

Washington, D.C.

“The chance to build relationships, tour the Pentagon and Capitol buildings, see the monuments, meet congressmen and lobbyists, and to be in the quick-paced atmosphere of Washington DC was a great experience.”
~ Dana Hazen, Alliance, Ohio

Each year, a select group of first year Mount Scholars travel to Washington DC over spring break to learn about leadership in our nation’s capitol. The trip allows for a unique opportunity to shadow a congressional office.

  • Senator Mike DeWine
  • Congresswoman Deborah Pryce
  • Congressman Pat Tiberi

Along with the shadowing experience, Mount Scholars have also:

  • Dined with lobbyists
  • Received a behind-the-scenes tour of the United States Capitol Building
  • Met with the Executive Editor of The Washington Post
  • Toured Arlington National Cemetery
  • Networked with Mount Alums in the D.C. area

This trip provides an extraordinary opportunity to explore the nation’s capitol and further the Scholars’ leadership skills.

Service

“Whether it is helping someone in need or just having a good time with others, Mount means that as long as you are willing to have someone’s back, they will always have yours.”
~ Dan Cunningham, Richfield, Ohio

Mount Scholars participate in a wide variety of community service projects focusing on social justice issues. First year Mount Scholars volunteer on a monthly basis to benefit the Ohio State society.

  • Fall Harvest Jamboree
  • Empty Bowls Hunger Project
  • Habitat for Humanity
  • Mentoring Fun Day with Big Brothers, Big Sisters
  • WOSU pledge drive

In addition, first year Scholars form teams and create and implement service projects for Mount Legacy Week. Second year Mount Scholars complete the Year of Service by volunteering 75 hours or more toward a service project of their choosing.





Mount Legacy Week

“Mount Legacy Week is the ultimate conclusion to your first year as a Mount Scholar. My group hosted kids with Down’s Syndrome and the best part about it was not that we made a difference in their lives, but knowing that they also made a difference in ours.”
~ Finolee Sinn, Hilliard, Ohio

Mount Legacy Week is held in early May. Each Mount Scholar joins a service team focused on a social justice issue. The goal of each team is to design and implement a service project benefiting the community, incorporating the Mount Essentials, and honoring the legacy of Ruth Weimer Mount.

Upper class Mount Scholars serve as advisors, guiding the first year scholars through the process. Teams present their projects and experiences at “Decision Night”. Scholars then vote on which team is most deserving of the Outstanding Mount Legacy Week Project Award.

Past Projects include:

  • Shack-a-thon, where groups of students built homeless shelters with given items
  • Fasting for the “30-Hour Famine”, with donations benefitting World Vision
  • Cleaning up the off-campus areas east of High Street
  • Hosting a “College Visit Day” for at risk middle school students



Year of Service

“Mount brought me closer to the Columbus community with the second year ‘Year of Service’”.
~ Rachel Koepsel, Cleveland, Ohio

Second years focus on citizenship and what it means to be a good citizen. In addition, scholars devote their year to serving their community. Scholars spend a minimum of 75 hours working with a service agency of their choosing, centered on a specific social justice issue. Scholars can also serve as Committee Chairs for the Mount Leadership Society as their Year of Service. Scholars incorporate the Mount Essentials as well as key concepts learned through a service-learning course, Ed P&L 271, into their service project.

Example Year of Service Projects:

  • Creating and hosting “Bucks for Hearts” Silent Auction, raising over $12,000
  • Serving as a Best Buddy for a challenged younger student
  • Coaching high school basketball
  • Building houses with Habitat for Humanity

In addition, the Year of Service is a great opportunity for scholars to grow as leaders and as people, to take advantage of new educational grant opportunities, and to carry on the legacy of Ruth Weimer Mount. Furthermore, the Year of Service allows second year Mount Scholars to serve as guides and role models to the class of first year Scholars.


Spring Break Service Trip

A small group of upper-class Mount Scholars travels to Huntington, West Virginia over spring break to build houses with Habitat for Humanity. The group stays in a local church and learns valuable hands-on skills. Ohio State is one of several colleges and universities that send groups of students to work on a house during the month of March. Hard work and manual labor pay off in progress on the house! Our inaugural trip to Huntington was so successful that we have made it an annual option for Mount Scholars.

Connections

“Mount helped me form relationships that have lasted throughout my four years of college. It was a strange feeling to find myself feeling more at home at college than in the house where I grew up.”
~ Anthony DiPietro, Wintersville, Ohio

Mount is all about relationships. Scholars learn to seek commonalities amongst each other while learning to respect and value differences. Mount Scholars are given the opportunity to make connections with Ohio State faculty and staff. In addition, upper-class Mount Scholars provide insight on the Mount experience and are encouraged to pave the way for the next class of Scholars.

Being a Family

“It sounded cheesy when I first heard it, but after joining the Mount Leadership Society I found that just as a family would be (and I cannot emphasize the word family enough here), we so evidently were ‘Together for Good’.”
~ Jesse Ondera, Jackson, Ohio

The Mount Leadership Society values connections. This program was designed to maximize the time that Mount Scholars spend with each other and to promote lasting relationships. Ruth Weimer Mount believed in creating and nurturing relationships.

Every second spent with each other is valued. They believe in handing down their knowledge to the next class of Scholars and paving their way for a better tomorrow.

Plain and simple. . . Mount Scholars care about one another. This is evident in their words and deeds. Mount Scholars are family.



Diversity

“One great thing about Mount is that it doesn't matter who you are or where you come from, each of our differences is what make the program so wonderful!.”
~ Ashleigh Hohenbrink, Leipsic, Ohio

The Mount Leadership Society believes in valuing individuals and developing leaders for a diverse society. This program is designed to provide a plethora of experiences for students to maximize their exposure to diversity. Mount Scholars come from all different backgrounds; we believe that diversity is more than “how we look.” Diversity encompasses our cultures, our beliefs, our ethnicities, who we love, and what we value.

Mount Scholars learn about diversity through leadership training sessions at retreats, classroom discussions, and intentional conversations. Mount Scholars are encouraged to take part in OSU’s Diversity Leadership Transcript Program.

Everyone is welcome in the Mount Leadership Society.



Mega-Mini Program

“Your mega will always be a resource for you and they will help you during launch week by showing you around to your classes and giving you some advice that you may have missed at orientation.”
~ Mike Salone, Hudson, Ohio

The Mega-Mini program is offered by Mount to pair upper-class Mount Scholars (Megas) with incoming first year Scholars (Minis).

The Megas serve as mentors to help the younger Scholars navigate the transition between high school and college. Megas are the Minis first “family members” in Mount and serve as great resources for the Minis as they begin to make Ohio State their new home.

Post Mounts

“I have come to realize that Mount produces students as well as alumni who whole-heartedly care about our school, city and nation. That makes me smile and it also makes me proud to be part of this program.”
~ Kaitlyn Mikola, Hilliard, OH

The Mount Leadership Society was constructed as a two-year program, knowing that Mount Scholars will continue to utilize their leadership skills to take on leadership positions within the university; however, “once a Mount Scholar, always a Mount Scholar.” Many upper-class Mount Scholars stay connected with the program after their graduation from Mount. Those second years who graduate from the program are considered “Post Mounts”, while “Mount Alums” are Mount Scholars who have graduated from OSU.

Mount Scholars learn about diversity through leadership training sessions at retreats, classroom discussions, and intentional conversations. Mount Scholars are encouraged to take part in OSU’s Diversity Leadership Transcript Program.

Post Mounts take on a number of functions for the Mount Leadership Society including:

  • Serving as Committee Chairs
  • Reviewing scholar applications
  • Facilitating workshops
  • Advising Mount Legacy Week teams
  • Serving as drivers for our trips

In addition, Post Mounts plan service projects, socials, and events just for third and fourth year Mount Scholars.

Post Mounts are a vital part of the Mount Leadership Society!



“A great thing about Mount is that it lives in you forever. Mount is more than a program you go through, it's something that becomes a part of you.”
~ Natalie Benson, Van Buren, Ohio

The Mount Leadership Society started in the fall of 1999. To stay in touch with Mount Scholars who have graduated Ohio State, the Mount Alumni organization was formed. The Mount Alumni organization serves as a networking tool, both for graduates and current students. Mount alums receive newsletter updates, attend semi-annual Mount Alumni events on campus, participate in service projects, and give back to the current Mount program.

We recognize the Outstanding Mount Alum at our Celebration Ceremony each year. We look forward to staying connected with all Mount Alums and hearing of their many successes. If you are a Mount Alumnus and would like more information on Mount Alumni activities, contact Kathy Krajnak at Krajnak.2@osu.edu.

Clustered Classes

“The clustered English classes fall quarter and the Mount retreat helped me to engage in activities and jump right in. I valued the diversity within my class and developed as a leader through numerous Mount meetings.”
~ Elizabeth Shoup, Orrville, Ohio

Mount Scholars have the opportunity to take classes with other Mount Scholars throughout their two years. These classes include, but are not limited to:

  • English 110 (First-Year English Composition), required
  • Education Policy & Leadership 271 (Leadership in Service), required

Clustered classes allow Mount Scholars to be in the same classroom with other Mount Scholars. Having another scholar, sometimes your roommate, to lean on for help is certainly a "perk" of the Mount Leadership Society!

Frequent Questions

Why should I choose Mount over a major-focused Scholars Program?

Many current Mount Scholars have faced this dilemma when applying to the Ohio State Scholars Program. With approximately 100 Scholars in each class, Mount will have students from varying majors. Because of this, there will be plenty of help available when it comes to classes. Also, if a Mount Scholar wanted to switch out of their current course of study, he/she would not have to risk leaving Mount. Truth be told, when asked at the end of the year if they were to go through the application process again, it was overwhelming how many Mount Scholars said they would make Mount their first choice.

Does Mount take up a lot of time?

Not at all! As a first year, Mount Scholars are only asked to be involved from around 10 to 15 hours a month. This includes attending leadership training workshops as well as participating in the monthly service projects. The fun doesn’t stop there, as social events are planned too!

Do you still have time for other activities while participating in Mount?

Yes. Mount encourages its students to partake in the many other organizations Ohio State has to offer. Mount Scholars have joined organizations ranging from Block ‘O’ to Greek life to Undergraduate Student Government. In fact, Mount Scholars are so well rounded that quite a few have gone on to win intramural championships!

What is expected of Mount Scholars?

Mount Scholars are asked to make a commitment to service. As a Mount Scholar, expectations are to:

  • Participate in all Mount activities
  • Fully contribute to Mount Legacy Week
  • Take ED P&L 271 – a Leadership in Service course, before Spring Quarter of sophomore year
  • Uphold the five Mount Essentials
  • Maintain good academic standing with the university
  • Remember the two areas that the Mount Leadership Society is known for – Leadership and Service
  • Make the most out of the Mount experience!

What social events are planned?

Aside from meetings and service projects, there are a plethora of social activities that occur. The sand volleyball courts outside of Halloran House provide excitement on fall and spring nights. Mount Scholars also participate in intramural sports throughout the year together. In addition, our “Member D” committee plans ice cream socials, birthday recognition, BBQs, and our very popular Mount Formal. But of course, with three other people in a room, and over 150 students living in Halloran, the possibilities of finding things to do are limitless.





How to Apply

The Mount Leadership Society Scholars Program is selective, and you must apply to be considered. Successful candidates have a variety of qualifications including solid academic records, strong writing skills, significant participation in extracurricular and community activities, and a diversity of life experiences.

Please note: Only new freshmen who begin at OSU's Columbus campus during the autumn semester are eligible to apply for the Scholars Program. Students interested in the Scholars Program must complete the Honors & Scholars Application after applying to the university. The priority deadline is December 15, 2011; the final deadline is February 15, 2012. To complete the Honors & Scholars application you will need your OSU ID, which you receive after you apply to the university; allow one week to receive this ID via e-mail after submitting your admissions application.You do not have to be admitted to the Columbus campus to apply to the Scholars Program; however, you must be admitted to the University in order for your application to advance in the review process.

For additional information about the Mount Leadership Society Scholars Program please contact Kathy Krajnak at 614.292.5768 or krajnak.2@osu.edu.
